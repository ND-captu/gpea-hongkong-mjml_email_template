From the movie Happy Feet, to the viral Hopper Dance with Stranger Things actor David Harbour (check it out here~), we all know that penguins love dancing!

Dancing on the sublime Antarctic. Dancing around the thriving Antarctic Ocean.

However, with threatening climate changes and an expanding commercial fishing fleet, we're afraid that the dancing party would soon come to a halt.

Get to know 5 penguins in the Antarctic

The good news is that 2018 brings an historic opportunity to create the world's largest protected area, a 1.8 million square kilometre Antarctic Ocean Sanctuary. The Sanctuary will help to protect wildlife like whales and penguins, as well as ensure healthy oceans which soak up carbon dioxide to tackle climate change.

But to do that, we need governments to stand up for ocean protection. In October, the Antarctic Ocean Commission meeting will decide on whether or not to support this landmark proposal. Their decision has to be unanimous. And this is why Greenpeace has recently launched the new Protect The Antarctic global campaign.

Together we can make history. Over one million of us have added our voices to a call to create the largest protected area on Earth – an Antarctic Ocean Sanctuary. But we need more people on board. We need YOU.

These seafloor submarine dives are part of an expedition by Greenpeace ship Arctic Sunrise, which has set sail to the Antarctic to carry out scientific research. Key findings will be shared with the Commission to strengthen proposals for marine protection in the Antarctic.

Because of YOU, Antarctic Ocean protection enjoyed a sweet triumph in creating the Ross Sea Sanctuary. But if we're going to avoid the worst effects of climate change and protect biodiversity, we need to safeguard more than 30% of our oceans. And the Antarctic is a fantastic place to start.

Let's make the Antarctic Ocean Sanctuary come true. For the ‘guins. For the oceans. For the planet. For all humankind.

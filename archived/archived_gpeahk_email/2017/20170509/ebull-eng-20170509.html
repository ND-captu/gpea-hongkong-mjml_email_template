Overconsumption of fashion is an international phenomenon. It is now deeply entrenched in our everyday culture.

A new survey, commissioned by Greenpeace, of international buying habits has found that we buy far more than we need and use. This is especially striking in Hong Kong, with two thirds of Hong Kong residents admit they own more than they need.

Our overconsumption is not only bad for the planet, it's also mot making us happy – most people say thrill of shopping wears off in less than a day!

The simplest solution to this is not producing, selling and buying so much.

Greenpeace believe that slow fashion can be innovative, profitable and sustainable, making a real contribution to our shared future on this planet. And the joy of life is ultimately defined by our relationships to each other and the connection we feel to the natural environment.

Buy Less, choose well, make it last!

Raymond Lo, the 65-year old then kayak and environmental enthusiast who did an 80-km around Lantau Island canoe trip back in October 2016 to raise fund for Greenpeace, is back!!
 
To refresh your memory, check Raymond featured in our Import Report 2016 Winter Issue:
 
 
Three years after, Lo, our respected donor, is turning 68. His age and being a cancer survivor doesn't stop him from challenging himself further. Lo is also inspired by Greenpeace's efforts in the Plastic-Free campaign. He yearns to bear witness on the plastic pollution crisis in our city's waters and to encourage more to take part in tackling the problem.
 
Lo decided to launch a 2 day-kayaking fundraising challenge in the hope of raising people's awareness about the ocean and the worsening plastic pollution, as well as support Greenpeace who have been fighting for a cleaner environment.
 
He will depart from Tung Chung at 7 am on March 30th. The 2-day journey is more than 100 kilometers, passing Cheung Chau, Lamma Island and Shek O. He is expected to finish at Central Pier No. 9 or 10 at around 430 pm on March 31st. Greenpeace will be there to say thanks to Lo. For that, we have prepared some booths and activities to tell you more about Go Plastic Free. You are welcome to join us at Central Pier at around 4 pm.
 
For the Ocean, Support Raymond Lo's 100km kayak fundraising action
 
One note to highlight about this generous act from Lo is that as he kayaks every day, he has a deep concern our ocean is getting sick. Recently he read Greenpeace’s microplastic research in Hong Kong waters and that adds to his urge to remind people of Hong Kong to try best in making the city Plastic Free.
 
We care about our water and ocean. We need to put further effort into making this city Plastic Free. Please spend some time check out this great effort of Greenpeace East Asia’s Research Team.
 
Check Greenpeace East Asia Report: "Microplastics & Large Plastic Debris in Hong Kong Waters"
 
Once again, give your support to Raymond Lo! And, hope to see you on March 31 at Central Pier around Pier 9&10. Join Raymond Lo & Greenpeace in fighting against plastic pollution and Protect Ocean.

It's time for real climate action

Since the Amazon fires broke out in August, our team in Brazil and around the world has been working hard to respond and continue fighting for forest protection and the rights of Indigenous Peoples and forest communities.
 
On Sept 25th evening, Greenpeace projected the calling to "Protect Lantau, Save Hong Kong", together with messages from the over 60,000 citizens who signed up for our "Protect the Lantau" campaign, onto the Government HQ at Tamar.
 
Here are some of the things we've done since the fires broke out:
 
  • - Documented the fires and spread images and footage of the fires to news agencies.
  • - Organized actions in Brazilian embassies in 17 countries to demand urgent action.
  • - Launched a global campaign to pressure fast-food giants, McDonald's, Burger King and KFC, and delivered our petition to Burger King Headquarters and to their UK CEO.
  • - Exposed Brazilian President Jair Bolsonaro's anti-environment agenda when he addressed the UN General Assembly.
  • - In Hong Kong, we did a projection on a big tree in Yau Ma Tei; calling for public attention on the issue.
 
 
We've helped amplify the pressure on Jair Bolsonaro and the Brazilian government, making it clear that there will be consequences to their destructive policies. While Bolsonaro tried to clean up his image abroad with a multi-million dollar ad campaign, we exposed his real intentions to expand agribusiness, mining, and land grabbing in the Amazon. Another important milestone was to successfully make the connection between the meat industries and the Amazon fires. Our campaign uncovered the role that companies McDonalds, Burger King, and KFC play in forest destruction so they take responsibility for the impact of their businesses.

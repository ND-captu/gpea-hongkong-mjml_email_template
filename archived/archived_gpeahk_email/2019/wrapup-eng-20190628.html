More from Hong Kong
 
 
Waste Trade Alert Greenpeace is urging the Hong Kong Government to have a plan to tighten waste trade and domestic exports and set a reduction target within a road-map for plastic waste reduction, otherwise, Hong Kong may become a new "international dumpsite". This was reacting to the returning of an illegal container from the Philippines to Hong Kong in early June.
 
>>READ Greenpeace International's press release
 
>>READ scmp's coverage
 
Protect the Lantau The funding for a feasibility study for the Lantau Tomorrow Vision plan is for now pushed back. We, however, will stay vigilant in safeguarding our environment from unsustainable city planning. Check out what we found from Peng Chau, an area that will be affected by the gigantic reclamation plan, with the help of Dickson Wong, a local naturalist.
 
>>VIEW Greenpeace Hong Kong Youtube (in Cantonese)
 
#PlasticFreeNow On 9 June, during a World Oceans Day & World Environment Day event hosted by the government, not only did Greenpeace set up a booth to support the plastic reduction in the city, but we also flagged the urgency of microbeads regulation to KS Wong, the Secretary for the Environment. In the following months, we will continue to need your continuous support in further urging the government to adopt measures to prevent the sale of products containing microbeads in Hong Kong. Thanks in advance.

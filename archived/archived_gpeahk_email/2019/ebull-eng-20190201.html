The Year of the Pig is beginning Feb 5. In Chinese custom, in preparing for the biggest festival of the year, people like to have a big clean up at home and at workplaces to welcome the beginning of the new year. All who are staying in town are also busy planning what to do during the festive public holidays. Here are some suggestions from Greenpeace:

Country Park off the land option for now.
 
Let's share two pieces of good news in this festive season. First, we look back to what happened a bit earlier, on the very last day of 2018 when the Land Supply Tase Force released the final report. With the support of 53,000 nature and environmental lovers, country park is NOT included in the eight choices of land options! Our next goal is to push the government to go for sustainable city development, develop brownfield and restrain from massive reclamation.
 
 
 
Google commits to first-ever solar power agreement in Asia
 
Secondly, about two weeks ago, a good boost to renewable energy development in Asia. Google has signed a long-term contract to buy 10MW of solar power generated from 40,000 solar panels in Tainan, Taiwan, in its first-ever Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) in Asia.
 
READ: Greenpeace statement on Google's renewable energy move in Taiwan
 
 
Greenpeace ships in Plastic Action
 
To close this email before we set sail to the Year of the Pig, some news about the Greenpeace ships in tackling the plastic crisis around the globe. Locally, we are going to release the findings of the microplastic research Rainbow Warrior first ever did in the Hong Kong waters at the beginning of 2018. Meanwhile, the Rainbow Warrior and the Beluga are deployed to tell the global story of where plastic pollution really starts and ends.

As we approach the end of 2016, we'd like to say a big thank you to YOU for sticking with us and joining your voice to ours so that we made many important wins in protecting our planet this year.

I recently read in the news that Japanese people chose the kanji character "kin" meaning gold or money as their word to represent the sentiment of 2016. I thought, if I was to think of a word to encapsulate Greenpeace this year it would be "blue", which for me represents the colour of a healthy ocean and the colour of a clear, clean sky. It signifies that pollution is no longer staining our water and our air, and they have been restored to their original and natural blue hue. This captures exactly our aim at Greenpeace.

How about you? What's your word for 2016?

This year I became a mum. I'm getting more concern about what kinds of things go into all our household products and I won't buy anything that has any hazardous chemicals, in particular microplastics which end up getting flushed into the oceans when we wash them off and then they harm the marine environment. Our generation doesn't own the oceans; we have to share it with future generations.

I'm sure many people will be making New Year's Resolutions. How about you? Why not pledge to make your life greener? As for me, my New Year's Resolution is to grow our people power. Together we are Greenpeace and together we will look after our planet.

I'm looking forward to seeing you next year!


Elsa Lee
Hong Kong Program Manager, Greenpeace East Asia

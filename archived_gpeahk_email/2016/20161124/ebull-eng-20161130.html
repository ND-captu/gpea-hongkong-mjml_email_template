Since the rise of fast fashion in 2000, we now buy twice as much clothes and wear them only half the time.

The volume of clothes produced is depleting resources such as water and land, challenging Earth's capacity to absorb greenhouse gases, toxic chemical and clothes waste.

Polyester, for example, is fast fashion's favorite material - it is used in 60% of our garments. Mostly produced in China, made from crude oil, it requires lots of coal energy and after wearing it, it takes many decades to degrade.

Today's trends are tomorrow's trash because recycling is a myth. Cheap clothing is poor quality, with low resale value, and there's just too much of it. 85% of all textiles are sent to landfill.

Our planet needs a break from over-consumption. Buck the trend. Buy nothing.

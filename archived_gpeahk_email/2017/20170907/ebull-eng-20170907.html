Our oceans are in crisis. A truck's worth of plastic is dumped into our seas every single minute, and with each passing second, more and more precious marine life is placed in danger.

While about a fifth of marine litter is made up of fishing gear and other materials lost at sea by accident, industrial losses, or illegal dumping, we know that roughly 80% of litter in the seas comes from land.

Once plastic is in our oceans, it flows on ocean currents all across the world – so even uninhabited islands in the Pacific and the Arctic are becoming dumping grounds for plastic.

Turtles, seabirds, whales - they're all being put at risk. It's time to say yes to a #plasticfreelife.

At Greenpeace, we're launching a massive new campaign to stop this, once and for all. But we can do this alone. If we act together - we can hold governments and corporations to account and stop our oceans becoming dumping grounds for plastic.

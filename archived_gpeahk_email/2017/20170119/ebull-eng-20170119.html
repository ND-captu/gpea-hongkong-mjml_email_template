Greenpeace has just revealed that HSBC, one of the biggest banks in the world, is funding palm oil companies who are destroying rainforest in Indonesia.[1] We've even caught the catastrophic damage on camera.

Forest destruction is helping to push orangutans to the brink of extinction.[2] It's causing forest fires which are linked to thousands of premature deaths every year.[3] Yet HSBC is pumping piles of cash into this crisis.

HSBC says on its website, "Considering sustainability when we make decisions helps us to protect our reputation...and manage the risk profile of the business." Now we've caught a palm oil company which was lent millions of pounds by HSBC, bulldozing rainforest in secretly filmed footage. Oops!

It's time to act.

Banks are extremely sensitive to scandal. And to make matters worse, HSBC often tries to trumpet its sustainability credentials.[4] If we stand together right now, we can expose HSBC's hypocrisy to people across the world, including its customers. We can force them to change.

We know just how to call companies like HSBC out. When thousands of us publicly shamed Santander by linking it to forest destruction, it cut its ties to irresponsible companies just weeks into the campaign.[5] Like Santander, HSBC doesn't want its customers to see burning forests and choking orangutans when they look at their bank card or bank statement.

The more of us who sign the petition, the more eyes there will be on HSBC as this shocking scandal is revealed. Please can you take one minute to sign the petition calling on HSBC to stop funding destructive palm oil companies?

These are words I hoped I wouldn't have to write. Last week, Donald Trump announced he'll abandon the Paris climate agreement.

In a move that's been called "one of the most reckless and reprehensible decisions ever made by a US president", Trump is trying to take a sledgehammer to plans to tackle the global climate crisis.

But if Trump thinks we're going to stand by while he puts our planet on a course to catastrophe, then now is the moment we'll prove him wrong. And that's why I am writing to you today. I need your help.

In the coming weeks and months we'll need to work together to prevent Trump's actions from rippling out and causing more damage. This could be one of the biggest challenges our movement has ever faced.

As you read this, Greenpeace's team around the world is already mobilising. Our office in the US has launched a huge response, urgently calling for members of Congress to oppose the president. In Europe, we're ramping up pressure on EU leaders to condemn Trump's move in the strongest possible terms.

It is clear that we need new leaders to step up and old leaders to renew their leadership. Here in East Asia, we are urging China to play a strong leadership role in all international negotiations on climate change, and to speed up efforts to move away from coal.

The good news is that China has already indicated that it is ready to lead on global climate action and expects all of the Paris Agreement signatories to stick to the agreement.

Comment: Can China really lead the world in fighting climate change?

Though the Paris agreement is by no means perfect, it's still a monumental achievement. A deal that hundreds of thousands of us across the globe tirelessly campaigned for. When it was agreed in 2015, it was the first time in history that the world's governments came together to set out comprehensive plans to tackle the climate crisis.

But Trump's actions don't just risk undoing hard work. While here in Hong Kong, climate change might seem like a distant threat, for many people around the world it is a life or death issue. In sub-Saharan Africa, climate change is triggering more extreme drought and pushing pastoralist farmers into an uncertain future. In Bangladesh, rising sea levels, brought on by melting ice and warming seas, are forcing people to abandon their homes.

With his announcement last week at the White House, Trump turned his back on one of the greatest threats humanity faces; and he chose to put more lives at risk.

While all countries must play their part in slashing greenhouse gases, the US has a crucial role to play in tackling the climate crisis. Without the US onboard, we must act fast to pressure other countries to ramp up their plans to reduce climate-warming emissions. Please donate now to help defend the Paris climate deal at this moment when it's under attack.

With your support, Greenpeace's ship the Esperanza is now on an expedition in West Africa to document the threat from overfishing to the marine environment, and to food security for millions of Africans depending on fish. Here is what our crew discovered:

"Sometimes we catch too much. The freezers are full of fish and there is no space left for more, so we have to throw away tons of caught fish" the captain of a Chinese fishing vessel told us. As it takes up to 3-4 hours to sort out the caught fish, most of the unwanted species will be dead.

My night on board a Chinese fishing vessel in West Africa

Meanwhile, many local fishermen resort to 'fishing from the sky' - the practice whereby artisanal fishing boats wait for trawlers to discard dead fish, as the fish are thrown overboard.

A story of empty nets and dead fish

During our joint surveillance with Guinean fishery inspectors, we found numerous bodies of dead sharks including hammerhead sharks, an endangered species, and manta rays aboard several vessels.

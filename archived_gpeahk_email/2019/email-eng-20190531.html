May 2019 Highlights
 
Loving and caring for the environment can be demanding and frustrating. Luckily when we embark on the journey together, we can bring about real change. We are happy to share some highlights from May with you:
 
 
üíöOn May 21st, Greenpeace activist Kin Wong reached the top of Mount Lhotse, the 4th highest mountain in the world. His mission is to raise the awareness of Hongkongers on the climate emergency by sharing what he witnessed during the expedition. Check out what Kin went through in his over-40-day journey from this Greenpeace Hong Kong Twitter thread.
 
üíöAfter kick-starting in mid-May, our 'Unwrap Our Supermarket' Campaign has been well received by the media, public and our lovely donors & supporters. What's more encouraging is that ParknShop has initiated selling more loose veggies and fruits through its three branches in universities. It is great to see greener supermarkets, check this Greenpeace Hong Kong Facebook post.
 
üíöLocal efforts to address the climate emergency: We had a full-house seminar on May 24th at Cheung Sha Wan with four speakers - Lam Chiu-Ying, Hong Kong Observatory former director, and local young environmental enthusiasts Andrew Yuen, Zoe Wong and Tin Long. We also braved the amber rainstorm and supported the local student climate strike on May 26th.
 
üíöThank you to everyone who has given your support to the Protect Our Lantau campaign...please do keep sharing! Brownfield is a cost-effective land option, instead of a mega reclamation project costing HK$624 billion and irreversible damage to our city and environment.
 
Til next month, let's keep making positive change through action and take care!

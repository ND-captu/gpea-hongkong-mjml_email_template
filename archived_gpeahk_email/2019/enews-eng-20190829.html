Forest fires are ravaging the Brazilian Amazon region. Here are 3 things you can do to help!
 
{salutation~HK Dear Eng first_name}
 
Forest fires have been ravaging the Brazilian Amazon region. From January 1 to August 20, the number of fires in the region was 145% higher than over the same period in 2018.
 
As soon as the fires broke out, Greenpeace worked to document the extent of destruction caused by the fires and deforestation. Here are some first-hand images to share with you:
 
 
The heartbreaking news may seem far away from us, but it’s closely related to you and I. This year, 75% of the hot spots occurred in areas that used to be forest in 2017. Forest destruction is directly connected to the climate crisis. Although wildfires often occur in Brazil during the dry season, they are frequently started deliberately in efforts to illegally deforest land for cattle ranching and soy plantations.
 
Here are three things we can do to help:
 
1. Consume less meat, and choose our food carefully
 
Brazil is the world's largest exporter of beef, comprising close to 20% of global exports, according to the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA). Growth in Brazil's beef industry has been driven in part by strong demand from Asia, including from Mainland China and Hong Kong. These two markets alone accounted for nearly 44% of all beef exports from Brazil in 2018, according to the USDA.
By consuming less meat and choosing better sources of food, we can minimize our impact on the forests. As consumers, our choices can help influence corporates and governments in the global supply chain to help end deforestation.
 
2. Share this information far and wide
 
The Amazon is home to many Indigenous Peoples and traditional communities. The world's largest rainforest is vital for keeping our climate stable. The Amazon fire crisis exemplifies the inconvenient truth that climate change and forest fires are a vicious cycle, with one exacerbating another. Sharing information on the climate crisis will help show that we can all do something to help!
 
3. Support the long-term fight for the Amazon
 
To save the Amazon rainforest from destruction and avoid the worst impacts of climate breakdown we need to make sure it is protected for good. But it will take all of us.
 
Greenpeace has been campaigning for an effective Zero Deforestation Law in Brazil which would drastically reduce deforestation and degradation of the Amazon rainforest and the Cerrado.
 
We also conducted an investigation and field research into the supply chains for raw materials (soy, meat, wood), exposing international corporates and governments that fuelled forest destruction by direct involvement or turning a blind eye to it. In addition, we worked with indigenous peoples and local NGOs for the defence of the environment and the rights over their territories.
 
With your support, we can protect approximately 3,000 of the world’s most critically endangered species that call the Amazon home. We will continue to monitor fire breakouts and demand the Brazilian government to end deforestation NOW.

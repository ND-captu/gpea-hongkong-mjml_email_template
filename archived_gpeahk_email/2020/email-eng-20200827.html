Climate emergency has always been our primary agenda, and this month no matter in Hong Kong and many corners of the world have suffered from extreme weather events. This Greenpost will highlight some of the responsiveness and work Greenpeace has done to combat climate change. Take this opportunity to thank you for your support to Greenpeace. Let’s share and take more Climate Actions NOW!
 
In early August Hong Kong Observatory (HKO) told us July 2020 became the hottest month in Hong Kong since records began in 1884. Heatwave alert! Greenpeace’s climate team, based on HKO data, found that the average temperature in Hong Kong in 2019 has risen by 1.5°C compared with the average from 1951 to 1980. Heatwave alert! (refer to the fiery infographic above). All things lead us to nothing but #ClimateAction!
 
The city was also hit by the first strong typhoon of the year. Typhoon Higos came, escalated shot up to increasing Gale or Storm Signal No. 9 and went in less than 24 hours last week. Learning from Super Typhoon Hato and Mangkhut before, Greenpeace lately produced typhoon safety tips leaflet (in Chinese only) about how to protect yourself during typhoons and act on climate urgency. We made it available online right before Higos hit the territory; and hard copies were sent to some District Councillors, who will distribute them to residents in communities.

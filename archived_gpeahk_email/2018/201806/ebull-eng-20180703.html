Did you know that in May, Hong Kong was voted Asia's most biodiverse city? Thanks to our amazing country parks, they are homes to many different and iconic species such as the leopard cat and the super rare Romer's tree frog. They're also incredible spaces that were specially protected and put aside more than 40 years ago for all the people in Hong Kong, including me and you, to enjoy, to find peace, and to breathe freely. How could we ever sacrifice that?

Well, the government is proposing to do just that. It's currently considering building on our country parks as a way to solve Hong Kong's housing crisis, even though there are more than 700 hectares of brownfields and other unused land in the New Territories that would easily meet our development needs.

Sign now to protect our country parks!

I love our country parks. And I'm sure you do too. They belong to the people of Hong Kong. They are what make Hong Kong so special and they are what make Hong Kong a liveable city. So how can we let the government bulldoze our home?

We only have a few months left – the government is currently running a public consultation on whether to open up country parks for development. Join us in telling them to leave our country parks alone, and instead develop brownfield areas and build affordable housing to once and for all solve our housing crisis.

Safeguard our back garden!

Once the country parks are opened up to development, they will be gone for good. Hong Kong is a densely-populated, high-rise city where many people live in cramped apartments and lead stressful lives. Can you imagine a Hong Kong without its country parks? Where would we go to relax? Where could our children go to learn about nature? Where could we go and simply breathe cool fresh air?

Don't let vested interests bulldoze our country parks. Let's keep Hong Kong beautiful! Save our country parks!
